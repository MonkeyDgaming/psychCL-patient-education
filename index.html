Patient & family education

A brain-and-behavior guide for changes in thinking, perception, mood, and injury.

Use this phone-friendly guide to discuss acute confusion, neurocognitive disorders, psychosis, TBI, mania, and depression while keeping evidence, research explanations, and teaching metaphors clearly labeled.

Start with the big picture Clinician notes

When to seek urgent help

New confusion, sudden behavior change, fever, severe sleepiness, trouble breathing, head injury, new weakness, seizures, or thoughts of self-harm should be treated as urgent medical concerns.

This tool supports education. It does not diagnose, replace emergency care, or replace advice from a licensed clinician.

Evidence labels used throughout

Three kinds of statements

Solid evidence

Findings that are broadly accepted in clinical care or supported by substantial research.

Likely / still studied

Research-supported explanations that are plausible but incomplete, variable, or still being refined.

Teaching metaphor

Plain-language imagery that helps people understand the concept but is not a literal brain mechanism.

How to use this with patients and families

Suggested framing

“I am going to show you a simplified map. The labels tell us whether a statement is established medical knowledge, an active research explanation, or a metaphor that helps us talk about it.”

Customization ideas
